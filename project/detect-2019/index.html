Anomali Detect 2019 - 'Guardians of the Cyberverse'

Project Details

  • Location : Washingtion D.C. USA
  • Started : September 29
  • Ended : October 2
  • Role : Speaker

My conference speaking experience

During my placement/internship year I worked for the threat intelligence company Anomali as part of the Anomali Threat Research (ATR) team. I was offered the opportunity to speak at the company’s annual conference DETECT. By the time I got to the hotel I had been awake for approximately 30-35 hours, so just a bit tired to say the least. I met up with my team and headed to the keynote where the main speakers included Admiral Michael Rogers, the former director for the National Security Agency (NSA). Being able to listen to such experienced individuals within the intelligence community was an amazing experience.

After the keynote on Monday, the subject of the first talk I attended described how ISIS had evolved its operations to increase their online presence as well as ensure secure communications between members. Afterwards, I went to a talk related to automating open source intelligence (OSINT) gathering. This was a very interesting presentation - OSINT work can be quite difficult to automate due to inconsistencies between different data sources and the unstructured nature of the data itself.

On the second day of the conference, my team delivered a series of presentations, so I focused on them to pick up some last minute tips on how to deliver my own talk. After an intense day of learning so many new things, Anomali held a conference party for all the attendees. The room was styled with a Guardians of the Galaxy theme. A fun way to unwind and socialize with my co-workers and Anomali customers.

On the final day it was my turn to present Ransomware - From Humble Beginnings to Monster of Masses. The narrative for my talk described the evolution of ransomware and how it could potentially change in the future. It was pretty nerve-racking as I hadn’t done any form of public speaking since high school. The conference attendees weren’t high schoolers but industry professionals.

Each talk had a 5 minute ‘Q & A’, where the speakers were asked questions by members of the audience. It was a first-time experience for me in this respect too - answering questions from so many people representing so many different skill levels, industries and organisations, including the U.S. military and other players in the threat intel space.

Overall, being able to deliver a talk and answer questions was a confidence building experience that has helped me to develop personally and professionally. I was very thankful to my managers and leadership for the opportunity to speak at the conference. I was also appreaciative of the support and encouragement of my peers who were instrumental in helping me prepare for the event. I would certainly encourage my peers to do the same thing, if such an opportunity ever becomes available to them.
