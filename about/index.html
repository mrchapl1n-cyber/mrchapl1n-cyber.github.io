A little about me

My name is Breandan McCavana, a final year computer science student at Queen’s University Belfast, Northern Ireland. Since my early teenage years, I always knew that I wanted to pursue computing as career, but didn’t know what area interested me the most.

I spent the summer of 2018 in the U.S and by chance found myself travelling to Las Vegas for DEFCON 26. I had heard about it before and thought it would be a fun to find out about hacking, as well as end my summer in style. It was an amazing place and the experience opened my eyes to a world of possibilities. I was hooked … a world in which people got paid to ‘break things’ was just what I liked - it is a community that I want to be part of.

After the conference I got more invovled in cyber security by going to different events and talks especially those related to penetration testing and offensive security. In some respects, I find the whole profession of hacking just to be like a never-ending video game where I am trying to get past the next boss and level up.

From what I’ve seen so far, hacking presents so many puzzles and problems to solve and I find that very appealing. I feel challenged when starting with a new target, and once I have pwned it, I am able to reflect on all the new skills I have acquired in the process. That’s incredibly satisfying to me.

You can find out a little more about my learning and development interests below.

Penetration Testing

Communication

I know the importance of effectively communcating issues found on an engagement to all audience types.

Python Lover

Open Source Intelligence Gathering
